Notes on contacting me

I view online messaging as an optional convenience, and not as a mandatory channel of communications. Consequently, I can be a little more difficult to get in-touch with than most! I've put together this page to hopefully offer some clarification on this.

TL;DR

Feel free to contact me, but I may be slower to respond than most.

If I don't respond or stop responding, see below for a possible reason.

Why am I so wierd?

Modern messaging and its norms present a couple of problems for me:

  • Messaging platforms allow people to contact others at anytime, including at times when some may not want to talk.
  • Read Receipts, common in most platforms, have fostered a culture of responding of soon as you've seen a message, even at inopportune times, for fear of being accused of ignoring others.
  • The low barrier to entry means a lot of pointless messages and smalltalk get thrown about. This can be fun, though having to respond to even the smallest of messages can get tiring.

As someone who is very introspective and requires a fair amount of time to myself, it can be more difficult to manage this. Consequently, I may at times limit what I respond to.

Ways of contacting me

  • Phone
    I reserve this for only those that need it. If you have these details, only call me if absolutely necessary, otherwise I may start ignoring you.

  • Email
    I use my email as a general purpose means of communication, akin to a messaging platform.

  • Direct & Group Messaging
    Treated similarly to email, though details aren't given out as freely.

  • Servers
    I tend to find servers or larger group-chats easier to talk in, as I'm more free to drop in-and-out when I please.

How to contact me

If it's important

Email me.

I respond to anything I deem important as soon as possible. This applies to most all formal, work, academic or otherwise urgent correspondence.

I may provide synchronous contact methods to those who I deem require it, however this is rare.

If it's not important

Feel free to get in-touch, though bare in mind that I can't offer any guarantee of a response time or whether I'll respond at all. Chances are I will respond, if a little more slowly than most

If you may have multiple ways of contacting me, feel free to use what you feel would be most appropriate.

Below are things that I likely won't respond to.

  • Smalltalk
    I likely won't respond.

    There are very few exceptions to this rule, such as if I don't see you very often. If frequent, I may stop responding entirely.

  • Unnecessary Phone Calls
    If you have my number and try to call me for things that aren't important, I may stop responding alltogether.

    I see phone calls as a demand for my attention, and get quite annoyed if that's abused.

    Message me instead.

You haven't responded and I don't understand why?

If I'm not getting back to you, and you're sure it's not due to anything above, here are some possible reasons

  • I have died
    Very sorry for any inconvenience caused.

  • I might be restricting my usage of devices/services
    If you contact me whilst I'm doing this, I likely won't see it for some time.

    Try emailing me if you haven't already, I tend to always keep an eye on that.

  • I might be away
    I may be on holiday or otherwise away.

    Sometimes I go camping, bikepacking, etc. During such a time I may not be in range of a network.

  • Network/Technical Issues
    My home network is notoriously unreliable. However, I will likely check for any correspondence elsewhere if possible.

  • I just don't want to talk
    At times, I might just not want to talk to many people. This isn't very common, and I'll still be responding to important correspondence.

    I have been known to do this for months.. However this is thankfully very, very rare.

  • I just don't want to talk to you
    As with anyone, this is always a possibilty. It may be temporary, permanent, or semi-permanent depending on circumstances.

    It isn't necessarily black-and-white, I may limit how much I'm willing to talk.
